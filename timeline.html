Welcome to the timeline of Chien-Shiung Wu's Life! ↓

  • 1912

    Wu is born in Taicang, Jiangsu province, China.

  • 1929

    Wu is admitted to the National Central University in Nanjing.

  • 1936

    Wu embarks to the United States to attend the University of Michigan. When she arrives, she changes her mind enrolls at the University of California Berkeley.

  • 1940

    Wu recieves her PH.D.

  • 1942

    Wu and Luke Yuan are married. She works at Smith College, then Princeton.

  • 1944

    Wu joins the Manhattan Project's Substitute Alloy Materials Lab at Columbia.

  • 1945

    Wu becomes an associate research professor at Columbia.

  • 1947

    Wu gives birth to her son, Vincent Yuan.

  • 1952

    Wu becomes an associate professor at Columbia.

  • 1954

    Wu becomes a US citizen.

  • 1958

    Wu becomes a full professor at Columbia.

  • 1963

    Wu confirmes the Conserved Vector Current hypothesis.

  • 1966

    Wu writes and publishes her textbook, Beta Decay.

  • 1973

    Wu becomes the Michael I. Pupin Professor of Physics at Columbia.

  • 1981

    Wu retires and becomes a professor emerita of Columbia.

  • 1997

    Wu dies at the age of 84, after suffering a stroke.
